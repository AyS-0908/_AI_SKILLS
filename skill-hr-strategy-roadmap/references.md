# HR Strategy Roadmap References

Sources checked on 2026-07-17. Use them to guide prioritization and employer-foundation checks. Verify official guidance live before country-specific legal or policy use.

## Strategy and Workforce Planning

| Source | Type | What to use |
|---|---|---|
| [CIPD: Strategic human resource management](https://www.cipd.org/uk/knowledge/factsheets/strategic-hrm-factsheet/) | Professional-body guidance | Link people management and development to long-term business goals; make individual HR practices coherent rather than isolated. |
| [CIPD: Workforce planning](https://www.cipd.org/en/knowledge/factsheets/workforce-planning-factsheet/) | Professional-body guidance | Start from organisational strategy; assess current workforce, future demand, gaps, actions, owners, data, and review. Adapt the process to organisation size and maturity. |
| [CIPD: People analytics](https://www.cipd.org/en/knowledge/factsheets/analytics-factsheet/) | Professional-body guidance | Use people data to solve business problems and choose only measures that support decisions. |

## Prioritization Rules

- Start with the business plan and employer obligations, not an HR best-practice catalogue.
- Treat workforce planning as numbers plus skills, organisation, location, cost, contract type, and scenarios.
- Put legal, payroll, data, and employee-relations foundations before advanced programs.
- Prefer 3-5 outcomes that the available HR and manager capacity can deliver.
- Use **Needed now / Add when / Not needed yet** and state the trigger for every deferred item.
- Review the roadmap when the business plan, locations, funding, growth, or workforce risks materially change.
